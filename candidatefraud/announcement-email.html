Most teams still treat candidate fraud as a résumé problem, and it stopped being one a while ago.

Our new research, The State of Candidate Fraud Detection & Prevention, maps how AI-generated identities, deepfake interviews, and workforce-infiltration schemes turned hiring into a security problem. A few things that surfaced:

People spot AI-generated content and real-time deepfakes about half the time. Judging authenticity by sight, a recruiter is flipping a coin.

A North Korean operative passed four video interviews, a background check, and reference checks at KnowBe4, a company whose whole business is teaching people to spot deception.

The single most reliable fraud signal, the device and IP fingerprint of a candidate’s references, is the one most teams have stopped using.

None of this means the front line is failing. Spotting a sophisticated fake by sight was never a fair ask, and even the firms that train the world to catch deception get tested.

Here is the encouraging part. The technology to catch this is getting built, and the market already has more capability than most teams are using. What’s missing isn’t better software. It’s a clear owner for the response, and a plan for the seam between Talent Acquisition and Security.

And that is good news, because the organizations making the most progress aren’t the ones with the biggest budgets. They’re the ones who decided, in advance, who owns it. That is a decision any team can make, and it is what we will help you work through live.
